## 3. Hard Answers (B1–B6)

### B1. The "causal" framing — defensible, or drop it?
**Drop every "causal" claim — and understand that the team already *decided* to and then failed to execute it.** The backend even computes the honest disclaimer `model.note = "Transparent distance-decay heuristic, not causal do-calculus"` ([spatial_engine.py:437](backend/agents/spatial_engine.py#L437)) — and then **never renders it** (grep over `frontend/src` finds zero uses of `model.note`). Meanwhile the word "causal" survives in **five** judge-visible places:
- `<span>causal nodes</span>` and `road dependencies` topbar metrics ([App.jsx:360](frontend/src/App.jsx#L360), [:364](frontend/src/App.jsx#L364)) — labeling a haversine-within-3km graph "road dependencies" is itself false;
- `Causal impact path` map legend ([App.jsx:572](frontend/src/App.jsx#L572));
- `Spatial-Causal Engine` agent heading ([App.jsx:652](frontend/src/App.jsx#L652));
- `causal_relevance` / `causal_context` / "Causal-aware retrieval" throughout [rag_core.py](backend/agents/rag_core.py) (it is TF-IDF cosine + fixed metadata weights — no embeddings, no causal graph);
- and the backend offline fallback literally names itself a **"local Bengaluru causal graph"** ([integrations.py:39](backend/integrations.py#L39)).

The one defensible "quasi-experiment" — the road-closure contrast — is **confounded the wrong way and worse than the team thinks** (see B3). So: **drop causal entirely; this strengthens you with a research judge**, who will otherwise (correctly) demand DAGs / SEMs / propensity matching and penalize their absence. "Transparent spatial-propagation heuristic with auditable priors" is honest and defensible. The team wrote that sentence in the code; they just need to put it on the screen.

### B2. The forecasting claim under a 40-min MAE — can you call this a "forecaster"?
**No — and it is worse than "loses to a median," because the live engine *is* the median.** `predict_clearance` → `_clearance_prior` ([spatial_engine.py:148-166](backend/agents/spatial_engine.py#L148)) serves `forecast_priors.json` medians with `cause+corridor → cause → supertype → global` backoff. That lookup is **byte-for-byte the baseline** `eval_duration.py` defines as the thing to beat ([eval_duration.py:81-104](backend/eval/eval_duration.py#L81)) — and the HGBR loses to it on MAE (43.8 vs 40.7). The trained model is **never serialized** (no `joblib`/`pickle`/`.pkl` anywhere; grep clean). So the deck implies a trained forecaster, and the runtime is an unbuilt model's baseline.

There's a second, sharper problem the prior reviews missed: **the backoff serves the *less* accurate estimator.** The team's own eval shows cause-median MAE **46.9** and cause+corridor **47.2** are *both worse* than global **40.7** on the held-out test — yet `_clearance_prior` *prefers* the specific tiers. The architecture's central design choice is refuted by its own validation harness.

**Strongest defensible framing:** "**Calibrated empirical-prior planning bands.** We validated with a leak-free temporal split, found that no estimator — including an HGBR — beats the global median on MAE, so we ship the simpler, auditable prior with an explicit band and sample-size-tagged confidence." That is true, rigorous, and rare. The team is **simultaneously over-claiming (a 'forecaster'/'model'/'causal') and under-claiming (hiding the eval that proves their honesty).**

### B3. The single biggest hole a judge finds in 60 seconds — and the cheapest patch.
**"Show me the model serving a prediction." There isn't one — the demo serves the baseline the deck says to beat.** A judge opening [eval_duration.py](backend/eval/eval_duration.py) next to [spatial_engine.py:148-166](backend/agents/spatial_engine.py#L148) sees it in under a minute: same median lookup, labeled "baseline" in one file and "forecast" in the other, with the actual model unbuilt and losing on MAE.

**Cheapest patch (~30 min, pick the honest one — do NOT wire HGBR under deadline; it loses anyway):** re-pitch the served path truthfully ("we ship the validated prior, not an overfit model") and add **one static "Validation" panel** echoing the eval summary (best-baseline MAE 40.7, ±15 min = 26%, capped 480 min, "HGBR tested, did not beat baseline on MAE") next to the clearance band. This converts the 60-second kill into a credibility *asset* by owning it before the judge finds it.

*(Honorable mention, equally fatal but needs 90 sec of clicking: the closure inversion in B3-adjacent — see Fix #4. And the `/allocate` idle-officer bug — Fix #3.)*

### B4. The unplanned / "sudden gathering" half — compelling, or coasted on?
**Coasted on, and this is the biggest *strategic* miss given 94.3% of the data is unplanned.** The reactive side ([App.jsx:382-593](frontend/src/App.jsx#L382)) is a *single-incident record browser*: you click a historical row and it runs the same spatial math. There is **no aggregation, no burst-detection, no "a gathering is forming" trigger anywhere** in the three UI files. Ironically, the proactive `ForecastPanel` is materially *richer* (band+confidence KPIs, predicted-vs-actual, feedback loop) than the half that covers 94% of reality. The problem statement specifically calls out "**sudden gatherings**"; the demo answers it with a search box.

**Make it compelling (half-day):** a spatial-temporal cluster detector — when ≥3 active incidents land within ~500 m inside a 60-min window, fire a red "SUDDEN GATHERING DETECTED near {junction} — run reactive analysis?" overlay. This is the single highest-ROI *new feature* because it directly answers the half of the prompt currently being browsed, not solved.

### B5. Honesty vs. wow — the one caveat costing a point, and the one place to out-bold a rival.
- **The caveat costing a winnable point:** the team buried their *best* asset. The leak-free eval (which even publishes where their own model fails) lives **only in CLI stdout** — `App.jsx` contains zero "MAE/RMSE/baseline" strings. They did the hard, judge-impressing ML-engineering work and then hid it. *That* honesty isn't costing them a point by being too modest about a weakness; it's costing them by being **invisible**. Surface it (one component) and it becomes the strongest on-screen credibility signal a Theme-2 judge can be handed.
- **Out-bold the bolder rival without lying:** a slick rival will claim "causal AI + ML forecasting + live MapmyIndia routing." ASTraM can demonstrably show it has *none* of those (haversine graph, median lookup, no HTTP client to Mappls exists). **Win by owning the inverse:** *"Every number on screen is auditable to a data tier and a sample size, and we publish a held-out eval that shows where our own model fails. Ask our rival to show you their held-out error and their model's failure mode."* The rival's unfalsifiable "causal" claim *loses* to a falsifiable honesty claim with any rigorous judge — **but only after the team scrubs its own contradicting "causal"/"connected"/"proof" labels**, or the rival turns ASTraM's own screen against it.

### B6. What would make ME pick this over a slicker dashboard with worse rigor — and is it visible?
**The force-allocation-under-scarcity story** ([main.py:321-373](backend/main.py#L321), [AllocationPanel.jsx](frontend/src/AllocationPanel.jsx)). Every real command center faces "I have 15 officers and 3 concurrent events," never "how many does one event need?" A dashboard that shows an over-subscribed force splitting — with explicit cuts — demonstrates domain empathy a prettier infinite-resources dashboard can't fake. **It is partially visible** (it's a tab with real demand/allocated bars and an honesty note) but **two things bury it**: (1) it's not the demo's centerpiece, and (2) **it's buggy** — under oversubscription it leaves up to *N−1* officers idle and allocates by *demand, not severity*, so the label "severity-proportional" is false (sev-10/demand-2 gets 1 officer; sev-1/demand-12 gets 6). Fix the bug, make it the climax, and it's the reason I'd pick this.
